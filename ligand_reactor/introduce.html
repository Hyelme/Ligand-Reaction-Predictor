Ligand Reaction Predictor

Ligand Reaction Predictor(LRP) is a website that helps the study of the ligand by drewing the predicted response results right away when new ligand emerges. Ligand means a molecule of Antibody / Hormone / Drug, etc. that binds to a receptor. When the body is taken as an example, receptors means to cells in the body,and a substance that reacts when combined with that cell is called ligand. When Ligand combines with a receptor, it generates various reactions.

The LRP is learned these response results from two learning machines in machine learning. And based on learning information, the predictor is executed to derive the predict response results when a new ligand emerges. The following are how LRP is used and definitions of terms.


How to use LRP:

1) Select the file containing the SMILES values.
2) Select the desired type of learning machine (CNN and DMLP cannot be selected in duplicated.)
3) Select the kind of learning method what you want in the selected learning machine.
   >> Action types are divided into two types: Agonist and Inhibition.
   >> Receptor types are divided into nine categories: catalytic_receptor, enzyme, and gpcr.

Receptor & Action Type Outputs in the form of  'Receptor_Action Type'
Action Type Output only 'Action type'
Receptor Type Output only 'Receptor type'
4) Click the 'Prediction' button.
5) After a wait time (approximately 30 seconds), the result is output in the 'Prediction' pane.


Difinitions of Terms:

SMILES  :  SMILES(Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) is one of the ways of which the structure of chemicals is represented as a character string, like WLN, ROSDAL, etc.
Agonist  :  A ligand that binds to a receptor and alters the state of the receptor that causes a biological response.
Inhibition  :  Inhibitor means all substances, including antagonist, that have a fundamental deterrent effect.
Catalytic Receptor  :  Catalytic receptors are cell-surface proteins, usually dimeric in nature, which encompass ligand binding and functional domains in one polypeptide chain.
Enzyme  :  Enzymes are protein catalysts facilitating the conversion of substrates into products.
GPCR  :  G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) are the largest class of membrane proteins in the human genome.
LGIC  :  Ligand-gated ion channels (LGICs) are integral membrane proteins that contain a pore which allows the regulated flow of selected ions across the plasma membrane.
VGIC  :  The voltage-gated ion channels and their structural relatives comprise a superfamily encoded by at least 143 genes in the human genome and are therefore one of the largest superfamilies of signal transduction proteins, following the G protein-coupled receptors and the protein kinases in number.
NHR  :  Nuclear receptors are specialised transcription factors with commonalities of sequence and structure, which bind as homo- or heterodimers to specific consensus sequences of DNA (response elements) in the promoter region of particular target genes.
Other IC  :  Other than LGIC and VGICs, other ion channels. It belongs that Aquaporins, Chloride channels, Store-operated ion channels to this ion channels.
Transporter  :  Transporters (membrane transport/carrier proteins) are specialized membrane-spanning proteins that assist in the movement of ions, peptides, small molecules, lipids and macromolecules across a biological membrane.
Other Protein  :  It belongs that Anti-infective targets, Bromodomain-containing proteins, Chaperone proteins, GEFs, etc. to this proteins.
